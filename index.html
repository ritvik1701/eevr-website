EEVR: A Virtual Reality-Based Emotion Dataset Featuring Paired Physiological Signals and Textual Descriptions

Pragya Singh1, Ritvik Budhiraja1, Ankush Gupta1, Anshul Goswami1, Mohan Kumar2, and Pushpendra Singh1
1IIIT-D, New Delhi, India
2RIT, Rochester, New York, USA

EEVR (Emotion Elicitation in Virtual Reality) is a novel dataset for language supervision-based pre-training and emotion recognition tasks

Introduction

EEVR is a novel dataset, containing high-quality physiological signals, ,electrodermal activity (EDA), and photoplethysmography (PPG) acquired using emotion elicitation via 360-degree virtual reality (VR) videos and subject-wise textual descriptions of emotions felt within each stimulus collected from qualitative interviews. Emotional stimuli are selected to induce diverse emotions across all four quadrants of Russell's circumplex model. The dataset contains data from 37 participants, each participant being exposed to a set of eight videos, with a pre-exposure baseline establishment and post-exposure semi-structured interview.

EEVR is the first dataset to introduce raw text and physiological signal pairs to support language supervision that objective labels cannot provide. Further, EEVR also offers baseline models for arousal and valence classification, including codes for data cleaning, feature extraction pipelines, contrastive pre-training and classification.
